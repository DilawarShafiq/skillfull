# Charge Capture Skill

## Overview
This skill covers the accurate documentation and capture of billable services, procedures, and supplies provided to patients in US healthcare settings.

## Key Competencies

### Clinical Documentation Requirements
- Ensure documentation supports medical necessity
- Capture all billable services rendered
- Document time-based services accurately
- Record supplies and materials used
- Link diagnoses to procedures appropriately

### Charge Entry Methods
- Superbill/encounter form completion
- Electronic charge capture via EHR
- Charge tickets and fee slips
- Surgical operative reports
- Procedure logs

### Revenue Integrity
- Ensure charges match documented services
- Prevent unbilled services (revenue leakage)
- Identify and correct incorrect charges
- Implement charge reconciliation processes
- Monitor for compliance with billing regulations

### Service-Specific Charge Capture

#### Evaluation & Management (E/M)
- Office visits (99202-99215)
- Hospital visits (99221-99223, 99231-99233)
- Consultations (99241-99245)
- Critical care (99291-99292)
- Prolonged services

#### Procedures
- Minor procedures with appropriate modifiers
- Major surgical procedures
- Global surgical packages
- Multiple procedure reductions
- Bilateral procedures

#### Ancillary Services
- Laboratory tests
- Radiology services
- Physical therapy
- Durable Medical Equipment (DME)
- Injections and infusions

## Charge Capture Workflow
1. Provider documents service in EHR
2. Charges generated from documentation
3. Charge review and validation
4. Charge posting to billing system
5. Claim generation

## Common Charge Capture Errors
- Missing charges (unbilled services)
- Duplicate charges
- Incorrect CPT/HCPCS codes
- Missing or incorrect modifiers
- Charges without supporting documentation
- Incorrect units of service
- Wrong date of service

## Charge Description Master (CDM) Management
- Maintain accurate code-to-charge mapping
- Update CDM for annual code changes
- Review pricing regularly
- Ensure compliance with payer contracts
- Document CDM change requests

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Charge lag (days from service to charge entry)
- Charge capture rate
- Charge error rate
- Revenue per encounter
- Unbilled services rate

## Compliance Considerations
- False Claims Act implications
- OIG Work Plan focus areas
- Stark Law and Anti-Kickback compliance
- Medical necessity requirements
- Documentation guidelines (1995/1997 E/M)

## Technology Solutions
- Automated charge capture from EHR
- Charge reconciliation tools
- CDM management software
- Real-time charge review
- Mobile charge capture applications
