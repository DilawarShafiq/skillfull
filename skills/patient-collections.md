# Patient Collections Skill

## Overview
This skill covers the collection of patient financial responsibility including copays, coinsurance, deductibles, and self-pay balances for US healthcare providers while maintaining positive patient relationships.

## Key Competencies

### Patient Financial Responsibility

#### Components of Patient Responsibility
- **Copayment**: Fixed amount per visit/service
- **Coinsurance**: Percentage of allowed amount
- **Deductible**: Annual amount before insurance pays
- **Non-covered services**: Services not under plan
- **Out-of-network charges**: Balance billing (where allowed)
- **Self-pay**: Uninsured patient responsibility

### Point-of-Service Collections

#### Pre-Service Collection
- Verify insurance and benefits before visit
- Calculate estimated patient responsibility
- Communicate expected costs to patient
- Collect deposits for high-cost services
- Offer payment plans for large amounts

#### Time-of-Service Collection
- Collect copays at check-in
- Collect past-due balances
- Process payments before checkout
- Provide receipts and explanations
- Discuss payment options if needed

### Patient Billing Process

#### Statement Cycle
| Cycle | Age | Action |
|-------|-----|--------|
| 1st Statement | 0-30 days | Initial balance notification |
| 2nd Statement | 31-60 days | Reminder with payment options |
| 3rd Statement | 61-90 days | Urgent notice, payment plan offer |
| Final Notice | 91-120 days | Final opportunity before collection action |

#### Statement Best Practices
- Clear, easy-to-understand format
- Itemized services with dates
- Insurance payment information
- Multiple payment options listed
- Contact information for questions
- Payment due date clearly stated

### Payment Options

#### Payment Methods
- Cash/Check
- Credit/Debit card
- ACH/Bank transfer
- Online patient portal
- Phone payments (IVR)
- Text-to-pay
- Payment kiosk

#### Payment Plans
- Interest-free payment arrangements
- Minimum monthly payment guidelines
- Automatic payment enrollment
- Payment plan agreements
- Default procedures

### Financial Assistance Programs

#### Charity Care
- Income-based eligibility (typically <200-400% FPL)
- Application process
- Documentation requirements
- Approval levels and discounts
- Community benefit reporting

#### Discount Programs
- Prompt pay discounts
- Uninsured discounts
- Hardship discounts
- Multi-service discounts

### Collection Communication

#### Effective Collection Calls
1. Verify patient identity (HIPAA)
2. State purpose of call
3. Confirm balance and services
4. Ask for payment in full
5. Offer payment plan if needed
6. Document outcome
7. Set follow-up if necessary

#### Communication Scripts
- Initial balance reminder
- Payment plan offer
- Hardship discussion
- Final notice warning
- Payment arrangement confirmation

### Bad Debt Management

#### Pre-Collection Actions
- Verify balance accuracy
- Confirm insurance exhausted
- Attempt all contact methods
- Offer financial assistance
- Document all efforts

#### Collection Agency Referral
- Criteria for referral (age, amount, attempts)
- Agency selection and oversight
- Placement file requirements
- Recovery tracking
- Recall procedures

### Regulatory Compliance

#### Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA)
- Applies to third-party collectors
- Best practices for internal collections:
  - No harassment or abuse
  - No false or misleading statements
  - No unfair practices
  - Respect cease and desist requests

#### State Collection Laws
- Varies by state
- Licensing requirements
- Communication restrictions
- Statute of limitations

#### HIPAA Considerations
- Verify identity before discussing account
- Minimum necessary information
- Secure payment processing
- Protect PHI in collection efforts

#### No Surprises Act (2022)
- Good faith estimates for uninsured/self-pay
- Dispute resolution process
- Balance billing protections

### Price Transparency Requirements
- Machine-readable files
- Patient-friendly price estimator
- Standard charges disclosure
- Good faith estimates

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Point-of-service collection rate | >90% of expected |
| Patient A/R days | <45 days |
| Bad debt rate | <3% of net revenue |
| Payment plan compliance | >80% |
| Patient satisfaction | >85% |

## Best Practices
- Train staff on financial conversations
- Provide clear price estimates upfront
- Offer multiple convenient payment options
- Follow up consistently but compassionately
- Document all patient communications
- Review accounts before collection action
- Monitor patient satisfaction scores
